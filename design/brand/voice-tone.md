# CertFast Voice & Tone Guide

## How CertFast Speaks to the World

---

## 1. Voice Definition

Our voice is the consistent personality that comes through in all CertFast communications. It's who we are, regardless of the channel or audience.

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | Definition | How It Shows Up |
|-----------|------------|-----------------|
| **Expert** | Deep compliance knowledge, technical credibility | We explain complex topics clearly. We know SOC 2 inside and out. We don't bluff. |
| **Approachable** | Friendly, human, not intimidating | We talk like a smart colleague, not a textbook. No gatekeeping. |
| **Direct** | Clear, concise, no fluff | We get to the point. We respect our reader's time. |
| **Empowering** | Confidence-building, supportive | We believe our customers can do this. We're here to help, not to lecture. |
| **Pragmatic** | Realistic, action-oriented | We focus on what works. We avoid hype and empty promises. |

### Voice in Three Words

> **"Technical friendliness."**

We're the engineer on your team who actually read the SOC 2 guidelines and can explain them without making you feel dumb.

## 2. Tone Framework

Tone adapts based on context while voice stays constant. Think of voice as your personality; tone is how you express it in different situations.

### Tone Spectrum

```
Casual ◄────────────────────────────► Formal

Support Chat          Blog Post          Legal Doc
Social Media          Landing Page       Security Whitepaper
Quick Email           Product Update     Enterprise Proposal
```

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone Approach | Example |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| **Marketing website** | Confident, aspirational, benefit-focused | "Get audit-ready in 90 days." |
| **Product UI** | Clear, helpful, actionable | "Connect your AWS account to start monitoring." |
| **Error messages** | Apologetic, solution-focused, human | "Something went wrong. Here's how to fix it..." |
| **Support/chat** | Conversational, friendly, efficient | "Got it—let me check that for you!" |
| **Documentation** | Precise, thorough, example-rich | "The `/controls` endpoint returns a paginated list..." |
| **Enterprise sales** | Professional, detailed, consultative | "Based on your infrastructure, we recommend..." |
| **Social media** | Casual, engaging, authentic | "POV: You just realized SOC 2 isn't actually that scary 🎉" |

## 3. Writing Principles

### 1. Lead with the Benefit

❌ "CertFast offers automated evidence collection."  
✅ "Stop screenshotting dashboards—let CertFast collect evidence automatically."

### 2. Be Specific, Not Vague

❌ "Save time and money."  
✅ "Save 400 engineering hours and $45,000 per compliance cycle."

### 3. Use Active Voice

❌ "Evidence can be collected by the system."  
✅ "CertFast collects evidence automatically."

### 4. Avoid Jargon (Unless Speaking to Experts)

**General audience:**  
✅ "We help you prepare for your security audit."

**Technical audience:**  
✅ "Our control mapper automatically correlates SOC 2 CC6.1 with your AWS Config rules."

### 5. Write for Scanners

- Use descriptive headings
- Keep paragraphs to 2-3 sentences
- Use bullet lists for related items
- Bold key takeaways

### 6. Be Honest About Limitations

❌ "CertFast handles everything."  
✅ "CertFast automates evidence collection and policy generation. You'll still need to implement the controls—but we'll show you exactly how."

## 4. Language Guidelines

### Words We Use

| Word/Phrase | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| **Audit-ready** | Our core promise—clear and specific |
| **Compliance automation** | What we do, accurately described |
| **Evidence collection** | Technical accuracy for our process |
| **Controls** | Industry-standard terminology |
| **Frameworks** | SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR as a category |
| **Your team** | Inclusive, not "users" or "customers" |
| **We** | Human, accountable, not "the platform" |

### Words We Avoid

| Word/Phrase | Why | Alternative |
|-------------|-----|-------------|
| **Users** | Impersonal, transactional | "Your team," "you," "customers" |
| **Leverage** | Jargon | "Use" |
| **Synergy** | Empty buzzword | Be specific about the benefit |
| **Game-changer** | Overused hype | Specific impact |
| **Revolutionary** | Unprovable | "Faster," "easier," "automated" |
| **Seamless** | Vague promise | Describe what actually happens |
| **Best-in-class** | Unless we have proof | Specific differentiators |

### Technical Terms

**Use freely (our audience knows these):**
- SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR
- Controls, evidence, audits
- API, integration, webhook
- AWS, Azure, GCP
- SSO, SAML, OAuth

**Explain on first use:**
- CC6.1 (SOC 2 control reference)
- Type I vs Type II
- Trust Service Criteria
- Drift detection

**Avoid or define:**
- Industry acronyms outside compliance
- Internal product codenames
- Overly technical implementation details (in marketing)

## 5. Messaging by Audience

### For Technical Founders/CTOs

**What they care about:** Time, accuracy, not looking stupid, engineering team focus

**Voice approach:**
- Respect their intelligence
- Be specific about technical implementation
- Acknowledge compliance is annoying but necessary
- Show don't tell (screenshots, API docs)

**Example:**
> "You didn't start a company to write security policies. Our AI generates SOC 2-compliant policies from your actual infrastructure configuration—not templates that don't fit your stack."

### For Non-Technical Founders/CEOs

**What they care about:** Closing deals, not messing up, cost, timeline

**Voice approach:**
- Reassuring but not condescending
- Focus on business outcomes
- Remove fear/uncertainty
- Emphasize support and guidance

**Example:**
> "Enterprise deals are stalling because you don't have SOC 2. We'll get you audit-ready in 90 days—with a 95% first-attempt pass rate."

### For Security Engineers

**What they care about:** Technical accuracy, customization, not being replaced

**Voice approach:**
- Deep technical detail
- Acknowledge their expertise
- Position CertFast as amplification, not replacement
- API-first language

**Example:**
> "You're the first security hire and you're drowning in evidence requests. CertFast's API lets you automate the tedious stuff while you focus on actual security architecture."

### For Operations Leaders

**What they care about:** Process, visibility, coordination, reporting

**Voice approach:**
- Workflow and efficiency focus
- Dashboard and reporting emphasis
- Cross-functional coordination
- Clear timelines and milestones

**Example:**
> "Coordinate compliance across Engineering, HR, and Legal without the spreadsheet nightmare. Everyone sees what's done, what's next, and who's responsible."

## 6. Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Website Copy

**Homepage:**
- Headline: Benefit-driven, punchy
- Subhead: Explain the "how" briefly
- Body: Supporting evidence, social proof
- CTAs: Action-oriented ("Start free trial" not "Learn more")

**Product Pages:**
- Lead with the problem
- Show the solution
- Include technical details
- End with clear next step

**Pricing Page:**
- Transparent, no surprises
- Clear tier differentiation
- FAQ addressing objections
- "Contact us" for Enterprise (but don't hide it)

### Product UI Copy

**Buttons:**
- Use verb + noun: "Connect AWS," "Generate Policy"
- Avoid: "Submit," "OK," "Click here"

**Empty States:**
- Explain what goes here
- Give clear next step
- Optional: Add personality (but not forced)

Example:
> "No controls mapped yet.  
> Connect your first integration to start automatic control mapping."  
> [Connect Integration]

**Error Messages:**
- Explain what happened
- Explain how to fix it
- Offer an alternative if applicable
- Never blame the user

Example:
> ❌ "Error: Invalid input"  
> ✅ "We couldn't connect to your AWS account. Check that your access key has the required permissions, or try reconnecting."

**Notifications:**
- Success: Celebrate briefly, then move on
- Warning: Clear about risk, actionable
- Error: Apologize, explain, help

### Email

**Subject Lines:**
- Under 50 characters
- Front-load important words
- No ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation!!!

Examples:
> ✅ "Your SOC 2 policy is ready for review"  
> ✅ "Action needed: Complete your AWS connection"  
> ❌ "URGENT: You need to do something NOW!!!"

**Body:**
- One clear message per email
- Scannable formatting
- Single primary CTA
- Personal but professional

### Documentation

**Structure:**
- H1: Task or topic
- Short intro: Why this matters
- Prerequisites: What you need
- Steps: Numbered, actionable
- Result: What to expect
- Troubleshooting: Common issues

**Code Examples:**
- Include complete, runnable examples
- Comment liberally
- Show expected output
- Test before publishing

### Social Media

**LinkedIn:**
- Professional but human
- Longer-form posts acceptable
- Thought leadership angle
- Engagement questions work well

**Twitter/X:**
- Concise, punchy
- Thread for longer content
- Technical tips perform well
- Don't force virality

**Tone check:**
- Would you say this to a colleague at a conference?
- Is it helpful even without the product mention?
- Does it sound like a real person?

## 7. Content Patterns

### The PAS Framework (Pain-Agitate-Solution)

Use for: Landing pages, sales emails, case studies

**Example:**
> **Pain:** "Enterprise deals are dying in procurement because you don't have SOC 2."  
> **Agitate:** "Every month without compliance costs you $50K in delayed revenue. Your competitors with Vanta are closing deals you should have won."  
> **Solution:** "CertFast gets you audit-ready in 90 days—at one-tenth the cost of traditional consultants."

### Feature-Benefit-Outcome

Use for: Product descriptions, feature announcements

**Example:**
> **Feature:** "Automatic evidence collection"  
> **Benefit:** "Connect your AWS, GitHub, and Slack—CertFast screenshots and logs everything auditors need."  
> **Outcome:** "Save 10 hours per week on evidence gathering. Never scramble for screenshots before an audit again."

### Before-After-Bridge

Use for: Case studies, testimonials, transformation stories

**Example:**
> **Before:** "We spent 6 months and $30K on consultants. Still failed our first audit."  
> **After:** "With CertFast, we passed SOC 2 in 87 days—and our engineering team barely noticed."  
> **Bridge:** "Here's how we did it..."

## 8. Examples by Scenario

### Scenario: Announcing a New Feature

**❌ Wrong:**
> "We are excited to announce the release of our new AI-powered policy generation feature that leverages state-of-the-art large language models to revolutionize how you create compliance documentation."

**✅ Right:**
> "New: AI writes your security policies in minutes—not weeks.  
> Tell us about your stack. We'll generate SOC 2-compliant policies tailored to your actual infrastructure. No templates. No consulting fees.  
> [See it in action →]"

### Scenario: Explaining a Price Change

**❌ Wrong:**
> "Due to market conditions and enhanced feature offerings, we have adjusted our pricing structure to better reflect the value provided."

**✅ Right:**
> "We're updating our pricing on April 1.  
> **What's changing:** Starter plan increases from €499 to €599/month.  
> **Why:** We've added AI policy generation and 50+ new integrations since launch.  
> **Your price:** Locked in for 12 months as a thank you for being an early customer.  
> Questions? Reply to this email."

### Scenario: Handling a Security Incident

**❌ Wrong:**
> "We take security seriously. An incident occurred. We are investigating."

**✅ Right:**
> "Security update: What happened and what we're doing  
> On March 10, we detected unauthorized access attempts affecting 3 customer accounts. No compliance data was accessed.  
> **What we did:** Immediately revoked affected sessions, notified impacted customers, and patched the vulnerability.  
> **What we're doing:** Adding mandatory 2FA for all admin accounts by March 20.  
> Full incident report: [link]  
> Questions: security@certfast.io"

### Scenario: Celebrating a Customer Win

**❌ Wrong:**
> "We are thrilled to announce that TechCorp has achieved SOC 2 compliance using our innovative platform."

**✅ Right:**
> "🎉 TechCorp went from zero to SOC 2 in 84 days  
> "We thought compliance would take 6 months and derail our roadmap. CertFast proved us wrong." — Alex Chen, CTO  
> Here's exactly how they did it: [case study link]"

## 9. Localization Notes

### French Market

- Use "vous" (formal) in product UI and documentation
- "tu" acceptable in marketing/social for startup audience
- Translate "compliance" as "conformité" not "compliance"
- Keep English terms for: SOC 2, GDPR (widely used)

### German Market

- Formal "Sie" for all business contexts
- Longer compound words are normal—don't over-shorten
- Technical precision highly valued
- GDPR = DSGVO (use localized acronym)

### General International

- Write for translation: clear, simple sentences
- Avoid idioms: "hit the ground running," "low-hanging fruit"
- Avoid cultural references that don't translate
- Leave 30% extra space in designs for text expansion

## 10. Voice Checklist

Before publishing any content, ask:

- [ ] Does this sound like a knowledgeable friend?
- [ ] Would I say this to a customer in person?
- [ ] Is every claim specific and defensible?
- [ ] Did I remove all unnecessary words?
- [ ] Is the benefit clear within 5 seconds?
- [ ] Does the tone match the channel?
- [ ] Would a non-native English speaker understand?
- [ ] Did I check for jargon or buzzwords?
