# The Compliance Roadmap: From Seed to Series B

Compliance isn't a priority when you're scrambling to find product-market fit. But ignore it too long, and it becomes a growth blocker that kills deals and complicates fundraising.

The smartest founders think about compliance strategically. They know when to invest, what to prioritize, and how to build security into their DNA without slowing down product velocity.

This roadmap shows you exactly what compliance looks like at each funding stage—from the bare minimum at Seed to enterprise-grade certification at Series B.

## Seed Stage: The Bare Minimum

At Seed, your focus is product-market fit and early traction. You don't need formal compliance yet, but you do need to avoid red flags that scare away investors.

### What You Need

**Basic Security Hygiene** (Non-negotiable)

- Password manager for the team (1Password, Bitwarden)
- Multi-factor authentication on all critical accounts
- Encrypted laptops and devices
- Basic access controls (Google Workspace admin, AWS IAM)
- Incident response plan (even if it's just a Google Doc)

**Legal Foundation**

- Privacy policy on your website
- Terms of service
- Basic data processing awareness (know what data you collect and why)

**Documentation**

- Security overview for prospects (one-pager)
- Incident response contact list
- Asset inventory (what devices, accounts, data you have)

### What You Don't Need (Yet)

- ❌ Formal SOC 2 or ISO 27001 certification
- ❌ Dedicated security personnel
- ❌ Complex security tools
- ❌ Quarterly access reviews
- ❌ Penetration testing

### Cost at Seed Stage

| Item | Estimated Cost |
|------|----------------|
| Password manager (team) | €200/year |
| MFA solution | Included with Google/Slack |
| Legal templates (privacy/TOS) | €500-€1,500 |
| **Total** | **€700-€1,700/year** |

### Red Flags to Avoid

Investors and early customers will ask basic security questions. Don't get caught without answers:

- **"Do you have MFA?"** → Should be yes on everything
- **"Who has access to production?"** → Should be limited, documented
- **"How do you handle security incidents?"** → Should have a plan
- **"What's your backup strategy?"** → Should exist and be tested

**The Seed Stage Rule**: Security awareness matters more than security perfection. Show you're thinking about it.

## Series A: Enterprise Customers Start Asking Questions

Series A is when compliance becomes a revenue issue. Your first enterprise customers will send security questionnaires. How you handle them determines whether deals close or stall.

### What Changes at Series A

- Average deal size grows to €20K-€100K+ ACV
- Procurement gets involved in sales cycles
- Customers request security documentation
- Security questionnaires become standard
- Insurance requirements (cyber liability) emerge

### What You Need

**Security Documentation**

- Completed security questionnaire template
- High-level security whitepaper
- Vendor security assessment responses
- Basic security policies (acceptable use, password policy, incident response)

**Technical Controls**

- Centralized identity management (Okta, JumpCloud)
- Endpoint protection (EDR/XDR)
- Cloud security posture management
- Vulnerability scanning
- Backup and disaster recovery testing

**Process Foundation**

- Employee security training program
- Onboarding/offboarding checklist
- Vendor security review process
- Annual security review cadence

### The SOC 2 Question

Most Series A startups don't *need* SOC 2 yet, but they start getting asked for it. Here's the decision framework:

**Start SOC 2 if:**
- Enterprise deals (€50K+) are 20%+ of your pipeline
- You've lost deals to "no SOC 2"
- You're 12-18 months from Series B

**Wait if:**
- You're still primarily selling to SMBs
- Security questionnaires are manageable
- Product-market fit isn't solid yet

### Cost at Series A

| Item | Estimated Cost |
|------|----------------|
| Identity management (Okta/JumpCloud) | €3,000-€6,000/year |
| Endpoint protection | €2,000-€4,000/year |
| Vulnerability scanning | €1,200-€3,000/year |
| Security training platform | €1,000-€2,000/year |
| Cyber liability insurance | €2,000-€5,000/year |
| SOC 2 preparation (if starting) | €5,000-€15,000 |
| **Total** | **€14,200-€35,000/year** |

### Red Flags Investors Look For

Series A investors conduct deeper technical diligence. Watch for these warning signs:

- **No security policies at all** → Suggests immaturity
- **Everyone has production access** → Risk concentration
- **No backups or untested recovery** → Business continuity risk
- **Previous unreported breaches** → Integrity question
- **No vendor security reviews** → Third-party risk

## Series B: SOC 2 Becomes Mandatory

Series B is the compliance inflection point. You're selling to Fortune 500s. Procurement departments require SOC 2 Type II. VCs expect security maturity.

### What Changes at Series B

- Average deal size: €100K-€500K+ ACV
- Mandatory RFP processes with compliance requirements
- International expansion (GDPR, ISO 27001 considerations)
- Board-level security reporting
- Dedicated security/compliance hire

### What You Need

**Formal Certifications**

- **SOC 2 Type II** (mandatory for US enterprise)
- **ISO 27001** (if EU market is significant)
- **GDPR compliance** (if any EU data)

**Security Team**

- Dedicated security engineer or compliance manager
- Clear security ownership and responsibilities
- Security roadmap and quarterly goals

**Enterprise-Grade Controls**

- Annual penetration testing
- Bug bounty program or vulnerability disclosure
- Business continuity and disaster recovery plans
- Data classification scheme
- Enhanced logging and monitoring
- Encryption key management

**Governance**

- Security committee or regular security reviews
- Board reporting on security posture
- Risk register and treatment plans
- Regular third-party audits

### The Multi-Framework Reality

Series B companies often need multiple certifications. Here's how to sequence them:

**Path A: US-Focused**
1. SOC 2 Type II (Month 1-4)
2. State privacy laws (CCPA/CPRA, etc.) (ongoing)
3. Optional: ISO 27001 for select customers (Month 6-12)

**Path B: Global**
1. SOC 2 Type II (Month 1-4)
2. ISO 27001 (Month 3-9, overlapping)
3. GDPR compliance (ongoing)

**Path C: Automated Multi-Framework**
- SOC 2 + ISO 27001 simultaneously with automation platform
- Timeline: 6-8 months for both
- Cost savings: 50%+ vs sequential approach

### Cost at Series B

| Item | Estimated Cost |
|------|----------------|
| SOC 2 Type II audit | €12,000-€25,000/year |
| ISO 27001 certification | €15,000-€35,000 (first year) |
| Compliance automation platform | €3,000-€12,000/year |
| Penetration testing | €8,000-€20,000/year |
| Security/compliance hire | €70,000-€120,000/year |
| Security tools stack | €15,000-€40,000/year |
| **Total** | **€123,000-€252,000/year** |

### Red Flags at Series B

Late-stage investors and enterprise customers look for:

- **Reactive security posture** → No proactive planning
- **Previous audit failures** → Control gaps
- **High employee turnover in security roles** → Culture problem
- **Unaddressed critical vulnerabilities** → Risk management issue
- **No disaster recovery testing** → Business continuity gap

## Timeline Planning: When to Start

### The 18-Month Rule

Start formal compliance activities 18 months before you need certification. This gives you:

- 6 months to implement controls
- 3-month observation period (SOC 2 Type II minimum)
- 3 months buffer for audit and remediation
- 6 months to use certification in sales cycles

### Stage-by-Stage Timeline

| Stage | When to Start SOC 2 | Typical Timeline |
|-------|---------------------|------------------|
| Seed | Not needed | N/A |
| Series A | If enterprise deals >20% of pipeline | 6-12 months pre-Series B |
| Series B | Immediately | 3-6 months post-close |
| Series C+ | Already complete | Annual renewal |

### The Cost of Waiting

Every month you delay compliance costs you:

- **Lost deals**: €50K-€500K per enterprise opportunity
- **Extended sales cycles**: 3-6 month delays for security reviews
- **Consultant premiums**: Rush jobs cost 50%+ more
- **Team distraction**: Compressed timelines stress engineering

**The math**: A single €200K deal pays for your entire compliance program.

## Conclusion: Build Compliance Into Your Growth

Compliance isn't a tax on success—it's a foundation for it. The startups that scale efficiently treat security as a product feature, not an afterthought.

Your compliance roadmap should match your business trajectory:

- **Seed**: Security awareness and basic hygiene
- **Series A**: Documentation and process foundation
- **Series B**: Formal certification and enterprise readiness

The key is starting early enough that compliance enables growth rather than blocking it. With automation, 90 days is all it takes to transform from "security questionnaire headache" to "audit-ready enterprise vendor."
