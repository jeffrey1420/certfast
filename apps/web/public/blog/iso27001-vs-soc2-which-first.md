# ISO 27001 vs SOC 2: Which Certification Should Your Startup Get First?

You're pitching to your dream customer. The deal is inches from closing. Then procurement drops the bomb: "We need to see your security certification."

Panic sets in. Should you get ISO 27001 or SOC 2? Which one closes deals faster? Which is cheaper? Can you get both?

This decision paralyzes founders daily. The wrong choice delays revenue. The right choice accelerates growth. Let's cut through the confusion.

## Key Differences at a Glance

| Factor | ISO 27001 | SOC 2 |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Origin** | International (ISO) | US-based (AICPA) |
| **Recognition** | Global, especially EU | North America, tech sector |
| **Framework** | Prescriptive (mandatory controls) | Flexible (trust service criteria) |
| **Audit Output** | Certificate + Statement of Applicability | Type I or Type II Report |
| **Validity** | 3 years (with annual surveillance) | 12 months |
| **Typical Cost** | €15K-€60K | €10K-€50K |
| **Timeline** | 4-9 months | 3-6 months |

Understanding these differences helps you match the certification to your business strategy—not just your immediate customer needs.

## When to Choose ISO 27001

### You're Targeting European Markets

GDPR compliance and ISO 27001 go hand-in-hand in the EU. If your ideal customers are German automotive companies, French retailers, or British financial services, ISO 27001 is often non-negotiable.

European enterprises view ISO 27001 as the gold standard. It's been around since 2005 and carries the weight of international recognition. Many EU procurement teams have never heard of SOC 2.

### You Need a Certificate, Not a Report

ISO 27001 delivers a certificate you can display on your website. For marketing teams, this matters. SOC 2 provides a report that only your customers can see.

If your sales cycle involves website trust signals and competitive differentiation, ISO 27001's certificate offers immediate credibility.

### Your Industry Requires It

Some sectors lean heavily toward ISO 27001:

- **Government contracts** (especially outside the US)
- **Healthcare** (complements HIPAA well)
- **Financial services** in Europe and Asia
- **Manufacturing** supply chains

If your target market includes these sectors, ISO 27001 isn't optional—it's the cost of admission.

### You Want a Comprehensive Framework

ISO 27001 is prescriptive. It comes with 93 controls in Annex A covering everything from access control to cryptography. For startups building security from scratch, this structure is valuable.

The framework forces you to think about aspects of security you might overlook: supplier relationships, equipment disposal, and business continuity planning.

## When to Choose SOC 2

### You're Selling to US Enterprises

If your target market is Fortune 500 companies, Silicon Valley tech giants, or US-based SaaS buyers, SOC 2 is table stakes. American procurement teams know and trust SOC 2 Type II reports.

In fact, many US enterprises won't even evaluate vendors without a current SOC 2 Type II. It's become the default security credential for B2B software.

### You Need Speed

SOC 2 Type I can be achieved in weeks. Type II requires a 3-month observation period but can still be completed faster than ISO 27001. If you're racing to close a deal, SOC 2 offers the fastest path.

### You Want Flexibility

SOC 2's trust service criteria approach lets you define controls that fit your business. A 10-person AI startup has different risks than a 100-person fintech company. SOC 2 acknowledges this.

You choose which trust service criteria to include. Most startups start with Security and add Availability or Confidentiality based on customer demands.

### Your Customers Are Tech Companies

Tech-native companies understand SOC 2. They know what to look for in a Type II report. They trust the framework because they use it themselves.

If your customers are Series B+ SaaS companies, dev tools providers, or cloud infrastructure vendors, SOC 2 is the common language they speak.

## Can You Get Both? Yes—With the Right Approach

Smart startups eventually pursue both certifications. The frameworks overlap significantly—roughly 70% of controls map between them. But sequencing matters.

### Start with SOC 2, Add ISO 27001 Later

Best for: US-focused startups expanding globally

1. Get SOC 2 Type II first (3-6 months)
2. Use the momentum and documentation for ISO 27001
3. Add the additional ISO-specific controls (risk assessment, management review)
4. Achieve ISO 27001 in months 7-12

This approach gets you to market faster while building toward comprehensive certification.

### Start with ISO 27001, Add SOC 2

Best for: EU startups expanding to the US

1. Implement ISO 27001 (6-9 months)
2. Leverage existing controls for SOC 2
3. Focus on the SOC 2-specific monitoring and evidence
4. Achieve SOC 2 Type II in months 10-15

The structured ISO framework provides a solid foundation, but takes longer to initial certification.

## Decision Framework: Market, Customers, Budget

Still unsure? Here's a practical framework:

### Step 1: Map Your Target Market

List your top 10 dream customers. For each, research:
- What certifications do they require from vendors?
- What's their geographic location?
- What industry regulations affect them?

If 7+ are US tech companies → Start with SOC 2
If 7+ are EU enterprises → Start with ISO 27001

### Step 2: Assess Customer Requirements

Survey your sales pipeline:
- How many deals have stalled due to missing certifications?
- What specific frameworks do prospects mention?
- Are there urgent opportunities that require immediate action?

Revenue urgency often drives the initial decision. You can always add the second certification later.

### Step 3: Evaluate Budget Reality

| Approach | Year 1 Cost | Best For |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| SOC 2 only | €10K-€30K | US-focused, fast growth |
| ISO 27001 only | €15K-€40K | EU-focused, enterprise sales |
| Both (sequential) | €25K-€60K | Global expansion planned |
| Both (automated) | €15K-€35K | Growth-stage, multi-market |

Compliance automation reduces costs by 50-70% compared to consultant-heavy approaches.

### Step 4: Consider Timeline Pressure

| Scenario | Recommended Path |
|----------|------------------|
| Need certification in 3 months | SOC 2 Type I |
| Have 6 months before major deals | SOC 2 Type II |
| European market entry in 9 months | ISO 27001 |
| Global expansion planned | Both, with automation |

## The Bottom Line

There's no universal answer. The right certification depends on where you're selling, who you're selling to, and how fast you need to move.

But here's what successful startups do: **They don't let analysis paralysis delay action.**

Pick the certification that unlocks your next 10 customers. Implement it efficiently. Then add the second framework when the business case justifies it.

Both SOC 2 and ISO 27001 demonstrate security maturity. Both open doors to enterprise deals. The worst choice is waiting six months to decide while competitors capture your market.
