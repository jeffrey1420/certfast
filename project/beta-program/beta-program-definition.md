## Executive Summary

The CertFast Beta Program is a structured 90-day engagement designed to onboard, activate, and convert our first 50 design partners into paying customers. This program serves dual purposes: (1) validating product-market fit through intensive customer collaboration, and (2) generating case studies, testimonials, and referrals for our public launch.

**Program Goals**:
- Onboard 50 qualified beta customers by Month 0 (Launch)
- Achieve 80%+ activation rate (customers completing setup within 14 days)
- Collect 200+ pieces of structured feedback
- Generate 10+ case studies and testimonials
- Convert 70%+ (35+ customers) to paid plans within 30 days of launch

**Investment**: €25,000 in discounts and program costs  
**Expected Return**: €126,000 in first-year revenue from beta cohort

## 1. Beta Eligibility Criteria

### 1.1 Ideal Beta Customer Profile

We seek design partners who represent our target market and can provide valuable feedback while serving as future reference customers.

**Required Criteria (Must Meet All)**:

| Criterion | Requirement | Rationale |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Company Stage** | Seed to Series B ($1M-$50M ARR) | Matches core ICP; decision-making authority with technical founders |
| **Employee Count** | 15-100 employees | Sweet spot for compliance need without enterprise complexity |
| **Industry** | B2B SaaS (fintech, devtools, healthtech, enterprise SaaS) | High compliance requirement; willing to pay for automation |
| **Geography** | France, Germany, UK, or Nordics | Target markets for Year 1; language support available |
| **Compliance Need** | Active SOC 2, ISO 27001, or GDPR project within 6 months | Ensures engagement and relevance; not "nice to have" |
| **Technical Readiness** | Cloud infrastructure (AWS/GCP/Azure), GitHub/GitLab | Required for automated evidence collection |
| **Decision Maker Access** | CTO, CISO, or Technical Founder as primary contact | Ensures fast feedback and decision-making |
| **Time Commitment** | 2-4 hours/week for first 4 weeks, 1 hour/week ongoing | Required for meaningful feedback and onboarding |

**Preferred Criteria (Nice to Have)**:

| Criterion | Preference | Benefit |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **Current Status** | Using spreadsheets/consultants (not competitor) | Easier switch; more pain to solve |
| **Enterprise Customers** | Selling to 1,000+ employee companies | Strong compliance urgency; valuable case study |
| **Funding Status** | Recently raised (last 12 months) | Budget availability; growth mindset |
| **Previous Compliance** | Failed audit or compliance delay | Higher motivation; clearer ROI story |
| **Network Value** | Active in founder communities/social media | Referral potential; advocacy amplification |
| **Multi-Framework Need** | Needs 2+ frameworks (SOC 2 + ISO 27001) | Higher lifetime value; stickier product |

### 1.2 Exclusion Criteria

**Not Eligible For Beta**:
- Companies currently using Vanta, Drata, or Secureframe (switching cost too high for beta)
- Non-SaaS businesses (e-commerce, services, etc.)
- Companies <10 employees (too early for compliance automation)
- Companies >200 employees (too complex for initial product)
- Pure consulting/services companies
- Startups with no cloud infrastructure (on-premise only)
- Companies requiring custom compliance frameworks not in roadmap
- Competitors or potential copycats

### 1.3 Application and Screening Process

**Step 1: Application Form (5 minutes)**
- Company name, size, stage, industry
- Primary compliance framework and timeline
- Current compliance approach (spreadsheets, consultants, nothing)
- Cloud infrastructure and dev tools used
- Referral source (waitlist, CPA partner, content, etc.)
- Expected timeline to purchase decision

**Step 2: Automated Scoring**
- Each criterion assigned points (Required = 10 points each, Preferred = 3 points each)
- Minimum score: 70/100 for acceptance
- Automatic rejection if any Required criterion fails

**Step 3: Manual Review (24-48 hours)**
- Review LinkedIn profiles of key contacts
- Check company website for B2B SaaS validation
- Verify funding status via Crunchbase/PitchBook
- Assess social media presence and network value
- Evaluate written responses for engagement level

**Step 4: Interview Call (30 minutes)**
- Confirm compliance urgency and timeline
- Assess technical sophistication and readiness
- Evaluate communication style and feedback willingness
- Clarify time commitment expectations
- Set expectations for beta program participation

**Step 5: Acceptance or Waitlist**
- Accepted: Immediate onboarding within 48 hours
- Waitlist: Kept in reserve for next beta cohort or launch
- Declined: Polite decline with explanation and future contact option

### 2.2 Feature Flag Architecture

**Flag Categories**:

```
stable_features:     # Available to all beta users
  - user_management
  - policy_engine_v1
  - evidence_collection_core
  - dashboard_v1

beta_features:       # Beta-exclusive, toggled per user
  - ai_policy_v2
  - risk_assessment_ml
  - french_language_ui
  - beta_integrations

experimental:        # A/B testing, 20% of beta users
  - collaborative_annotations
  - custom_control_builder
  - evidence_auto_collection_full

disabled:            # Not available in beta
  - iso27001_module
  - gdpr_full_module
  - mobile_app
```

**Rollout Strategy**:
- **Week 1-2**: 20% of beta users get experimental features (A group)
- **Week 3-4**: Compare metrics, expand to 50% if positive
- **Week 5-8**: Full rollout of validated features to all beta users
- **Week 9+**: Iterate based on feedback, prepare v1.0 feature set

### 2.3 Beta-to-v1.0 Transition Plan

**30 Days Before Launch**:
- Freeze new beta features
- Focus on bug fixes and stability
- Begin migration planning for beta users

**Launch Day**:
- All stable beta features become v1.0 features
- Beta-exclusive features evaluated for v1.0 inclusion based on feedback
- Beta users grandfathered into v1.0 with special pricing

**Post-Launch (30 days)**:
- Beta users converted to paid plans
- Beta-exclusive features either promoted to v1.0 or sunsetted
- Feedback from beta directly informs v1.1 roadmap

## 3. Feedback Collection Process

### 3.1 Feedback Collection Framework

**Multi-Channel Feedback System**:

| Channel | Frequency | Purpose | Owner |
|---------|-----------|---------|-------|
| **In-App NPS/CSAT** | Weekly pulse | Sentiment tracking | Automated |
| **Feature Requests** | Continuous | Product roadmap input | Product Team |
| **Bug Reports** | Immediate | Stability improvements | Engineering |
| **Weekly Check-ins** | Weekly (first 4 weeks) | Relationship building, blockers | Customer Success |
| **Monthly Survey** | Monthly | Structured feedback, NPS | Product Team |
| **Quarterly Interview** | Once per quarter | Deep qualitative insights | Founder/PM |
| **Slack Community** | Continuous | Peer support, rapid feedback | Community Manager |
| **Usage Analytics** | Continuous | Behavioral data, engagement | Data Team |

### 3.2 Weekly Check-in Structure (Weeks 1-4)

**Call Agenda (30 minutes)**:

1. **Progress Review** (5 min)
   - Compliance milestones achieved this week
   - Evidence collection status
   - Blockers or challenges encountered

2. **Product Feedback** (15 min)
   - What's working well?
   - What's confusing or frustrating?
   - Missing features or capabilities?
   - UI/UX observations

3. **Support Needs** (5 min)
   - Questions about compliance process
   - Integration troubleshooting
   - Policy customization requests

4. **Next Week Planning** (5 min)
   - Goals for next week
   - Upcoming milestones
   - Scheduling next check-in

**Documentation**:
- Notes recorded in HubSpot/CRM
- Tag feedback by category (bug, feature request, UX issue, compliance question)
- Track sentiment trend (positive, neutral, negative)
- Escalate critical issues within 24 hours

### 3.3 Monthly Survey Template

**Survey Structure** (10 questions, 5 minutes)**:

1. **Net Promoter Score**: "How likely are you to recommend CertFast to another startup?" (0-10)

2. **Product-Market Fit**: "How would you feel if you could no longer use CertFast?" (Very disappointed / Somewhat disappointed / Not disappointed)

3. **Feature Satisfaction**: Rate satisfaction with (1-5 scale):
   - Policy generation
   - Evidence collection
   - Dashboard/analytics
   - Integrations
   - Support quality

4. **Value Realization**: "Has CertFast saved you time/money on compliance?" (Yes, significantly / Yes, somewhat / No / Too early to tell)

5. **Competitive Comparison**: "How does CertFast compare to alternatives you considered?" (Much better / Better / Similar / Worse)

6. **Blockers**: "What's preventing you from achieving compliance faster?" (Open text)

7. **Missing Features**: "What feature would make CertFast indispensable?" (Open text)

8. **Likelihood to Purchase**: "How likely are you to become a paying customer at launch?" (Definitely / Probably / Unsure / Probably not / Definitely not)

9. **Referral Willingness**: "Would you be willing to provide a testimonial or case study?" (Yes / Maybe / No)

10. **Open Feedback**: "Any other feedback or suggestions?" (Open text)

### 3.4 Feedback Analysis and Action

**Weekly Feedback Review Meeting** (Mondays, 30 minutes):
- Attendees: Product Manager, Engineering Lead, Customer Success Lead
- Review all feedback collected previous week
- Categorize and prioritize issues
- Assign owners for follow-up

**Feedback Priority Matrix**:

| Impact | Frequency | Action | Timeline |

| High | High | Immediate fix | 24-48 hours |
| High | Low | Quick win | 1 week |
| Low | High | Planned fix | 2-4 weeks |

### 4.2 Success Metrics and Health Scoring

**Beta Customer Health Score** (0-100):

| Factor | Weight | Calculation |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Product Usage** | 30% | Logins/week × feature breadth (0-30 pts) |
| **Evidence Progress** | 25% | % of controls with evidence (0-25 pts) |
| **Engagement** | 20% | Meeting attendance + survey response (0-20 pts) |
| **Sentiment** | 15% | NPS + CSAT scores (0-15 pts) |
| **Compliance Progress** | 10% | Milestones achieved (0-10 pts) |

**Health Score Interpretation**:
- **Green (80-100)**: Healthy, likely to convert, potential advocate
- **Yellow (50-79)**: At-risk, needs attention, intervention required
- **Red (0-49)**: Critical, likely to churn, salvage or replace

**Intervention Triggers**:
- No login for 7 days → Automated re-engagement email
- Health score drops to Yellow → CS manager check-in within 48 hours
- Health score drops to Red → Founder escalation call within 24 hours
- Negative NPS (<7) → Product manager interview within 1 week

### 4.3 Common Scenarios and Playbooks

**Scenario 1: Integration Challenges**

*Symptoms*: Customer stuck on AWS/GitHub integration, low evidence collection  
*Intervention*:
1. Technical support call within 24 hours
2. Screen share to debug integration
3. Alternative integration options if primary fails
4. Manual evidence upload training as fallback
5. Engineering ticket if bug identified

**Scenario 2: Low Engagement**

*Symptoms*: Missing meetings, delayed responses, low product usage  
*Intervention*:
1. Personalized email from founder
2. Reschedule for more convenient time
3. Identify underlying issue (not ready, too busy, lost interest)
4. Adjust engagement model (async vs. sync)
5. Consider replacement if no improvement after 2 weeks

**Scenario 3: Feature Gap Anxiety**

*Symptoms*: "I need [feature X] that's not available"  
*Intervention*:
1. Validate if blocker or nice-to-have
2. If blocker: Offer workaround or manual assistance
3. If v1.0 feature: Confirm timeline and provide early access
4. If post-v1.0: Explain roadmap, offer interim solution
5. Document for roadmap prioritization

**Scenario 4: Compliance Timeline Pressure**

*Symptoms*: Audit scheduled soon, worried about readiness  
*Intervention*:
1. Emergency compliance sprint plan
2. Prioritize controls for their specific audit
3. Daily check-ins during final weeks
4. Direct auditor introduction and preparation
5. Offer consultant support if needed
