**Version**: 1.0

## 1. Executive Summary

This playbook provides everything needed to successfully sell CertFast to European startups and scaleups. CertFast is a compliance automation platform that helps companies achieve SOC 2, ISO 27001, and GDPR compliance in weeks instead of months.

**Our Mission**: Make enterprise-grade compliance accessible to every European startup.

**Sales Philosophy**: Consultative selling focused on risk reduction and business enablement. We don't sell compliance—we sell the ability to close enterprise deals faster.

## 2. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### 2.1 Firmographic Criteria

| Attribute | Ideal | Acceptable | Avoid |
|-----------|-------|------------|-------|
| **Company Stage** | Seed to Series B | Series C, Bootstrapped | Pre-seed, Enterprise |
| **Employee Count** | 10-100 | 5-10, 100-250 | <5, >500 |
| **Annual Revenue** | €500K - €10M | €100K - €500K | Pre-revenue |
| **Industry** | B2B SaaS, Fintech, HealthTech | Marketplace, Proptech | B2C, Non-tech |
| **Geography** | EU/EEA, UK | Switzerland, Norway | Outside Europe |
| **Target Buyers** | Enterprise/SMB | Mid-market only | Consumer-only |

### 2.2 Behavioral Signals (High Intent)

**Strong Signals (Prioritize These):**
- Recently raised funding (last 6 months)
- Hiring for security/compliance roles
- Active enterprise sales cycles
- Lost deals due to security questionnaires
- Expanding into regulated industries
- SOC 2 or ISO 27001 mentioned in job postings
- Currently using manual compliance processes (spreadsheets, shared drives)

**Moderate Signals:**
- Growing team rapidly (20%+ headcount growth)
- Expanding to new markets
- Recent data breach in industry
- Customer security requirements increasing

### 2.3 Decision-Making Unit (DMU)

**Primary Decision Maker: CTO/VP Engineering (40% of deals)**
- Pain: Technical implementation burden, audit preparation
- Motivation: Reduce engineering overhead, ship faster
- Language: Automation, APIs, integrations, developer experience

**Primary Decision Maker: CEO/Founder (35% of deals)**
- Pain: Lost revenue from compliance delays, enterprise deals stuck
- Motivation: Close bigger customers, reduce risk
- Language: Revenue acceleration, risk mitigation, competitive advantage

**Influencer: CISO/Security Lead (15% of deals)**
- Pain: Overwhelmed by compliance scope, limited resources
- Motivation: Professional credibility, efficient program
- Language: Framework alignment, evidence collection, auditor acceptance

**Economic Buyer: CFO/COO (10% of deals)**
- Pain: Unexpected compliance costs, audit failures
- Motivation: Budget predictability, board confidence
- Language: ROI, cost avoidance, predictable timeline

### 2.4 ICP Company Archetypes

**Archetype 1: "The Enterprise-Aspirant"**
- B2B SaaS startup landing first enterprise logos
- 20-50 employees, €1M-€5M ARR
- Currently using manual security questionnaires
- Just lost a deal to a competitor with SOC 2
- Timeline: 3-6 months to first audit

**Archetype 2: "The Rapid Scaler"**
- High-growth startup expanding internationally
- 50-150 employees, Series A/B funded
- Compliance is blocking US market entry
- Has budget allocated for compliance
- Timeline: 1-3 months to audit readiness

**Archetype 3: "The Compliance Convert"**
- Established startup responding to customer pressure
- Multiple enterprise customers asking for certifications
- Currently working with expensive consultants
- Frustrated with slow progress
- Timeline: Immediate need

## 3. Discovery Call Script

### 3.1 Pre-Call Preparation (15 minutes)

**Research Checklist:**
- [ ] Review company website and product
- [ ] Check LinkedIn for recent news/funding
- [ ] Look for job postings mentioning compliance
- [ ] Review their customer base (enterprise signals?)
- [ ] Check if they have any existing certifications
- [ ] Look at competitor landscape

**Tools to Use:**
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator
- Crunchbase (funding data)
- Company website careers page
- G2/Capterra reviews (if available)

### 3.2 Discovery Call Structure (30-45 minutes)

**Opening (3 minutes)**
```
"Thanks for taking the time today, [Name]. Before we dive in, 
I want to make sure I'm respectful of your time. I scheduled 
45 minutes—does that still work for you?

Great. To give you context on why I reached out: we work with 
European B2B startups who are growing fast but getting stuck 
in enterprise sales cycles because of compliance requirements. 
Companies like [relevant customer/competitor] use CertFast to 
get SOC 2 or ISO 27001 certified in weeks instead of months.

But I'm not here to pitch you today. I want to understand what 
you're dealing with and see if there's a fit. Sound good?"
```

**Context Setting Questions (5 minutes)**
```
"Let me start with some context:

1. Can you tell me a bit about [Company]'s current stage and 
   primary market?
   
2. Who are your typical customers? Are you selling to enterprise, 
   mid-market, or SMB?
   
3. What's driving the compliance conversation right now? Is it 
   customer pressure, a specific deal, expansion plans, or 
   something else?"
```

**Pain Discovery (15 minutes)**
```
"Now let's dig into the compliance side:

4. Walk me through what happens today when an enterprise prospect 
   asks for your security documentation. How do you handle that?
   [Listen for: manual processes, delays, lost deals]

5. Have you lost any deals or had deals stalled specifically 
   because of compliance or security requirements?
   [If yes] Tell me about that—what happened?

6. What's your current approach to compliance? Are you working 
   with consultants, doing it internally, or is this the first 
   time you're looking at it?
   
7. If you've started the process, where are you getting stuck? 
   [Listen for: documentation, evidence collection, policy gaps, 
   auditor communication]

8. What's the cost of not solving this in the next 3-6 months? 
   [Listen for: lost revenue, delayed deals, competitive pressure]"
```

**Timeline & Budget Discovery (10 minutes)**
```
"Let's talk about getting this done:

9. Is there a specific event driving your timeline? A big deal, 
   funding milestone, market expansion?

10. What does success look like for you? Having the certificate 
    in hand, or being audit-ready, or something else?

11. Have you allocated budget for compliance this year? 
    [Don't ask for specific numbers—listen for signals]

12. Who else needs to be involved in this decision? 
    [Map the DMU]"
```

**Value Alignment & Next Steps (5 minutes)**
```
"Based on what you've shared, it sounds like [summarize their 
situation]. Does that sound right?

[If qualified]
From what I'm hearing, there are a few ways we could help:
- [Specific pain point 1] → [CertFast solution]
- [Specific pain point 2] → [CertFast solution]

The typical timeline for a company in your situation is [X] weeks 
to [audit-ready/certification]. Does that align with what you need?

I'd recommend we do a quick demo tailored to your use case. I can 
show you exactly how we'd handle [specific pain point they mentioned]. 
Does [day] at [time] work for you and [other stakeholder]?"

[If not qualified]
Based on what you've shared, it sounds like compliance might not be 
your top priority right now. That's totally fine. Can I check back 
in [timeframe] when [trigger event] might be more relevant?"
```

## 4. Demo Script

### 4.1 Pre-Demo Preparation

**Demo Environment Setup:**
- Use demo.certfast.io (never production)
- Have 3-5 pre-configured demo scenarios ready
- Test all features 30 minutes before demo
- Have backup slides ready for technical issues

**Customization Based on Discovery:**
- Lead with the pain point they emphasized most
- Prepare evidence of similar customer success
- Research their tech stack for integration relevance

### 4.2 Demo Structure (30-45 minutes)

**Opening & Agenda (3 minutes)**
```
"Before we jump in, let me confirm what you want to see today. 
Based on our last conversation, [Name] mentioned [primary pain point] 
is your biggest concern, and [Name] is interested in seeing [secondary 
interest]. Did I get that right?

Great. Here's how we'll use our time:
- First 5 minutes: Quick context on CertFast approach
- Next 25 minutes: Live demo focused on your use cases
- Last 10 minutes: Q&A and next steps

Also, I want this to be interactive—if you have questions, stop me 
anytime. And if I start showing something that isn't relevant, tell 
me to skip it. Deal?"
```

**The Hook: Problem Validation (5 minutes)**
```
"Let me start with a story. Most startups we talk to are in one of 
two situations:

Either they're spending 6-12 months trying to get compliant—hiring 
consultants, writing policies from scratch, manually collecting 
evidence—and the whole time, enterprise deals are on hold.

Or they think they're compliant because they filled out a checklist, 
then the auditor comes in and they fail because they can't prove 
anything.

Which of those resonates more with where you are today?"

[Let them respond, acknowledge their situation]

"The reason this happens is that compliance isn't about checking 
boxes—it's about proving you have the right controls in place, 
continuously. That's what CertFast automates."
```

**Demo Flow: The Journey**

**Stop 1: Dashboard & Overview (3 minutes)**
```
"This is what you see when you log in. The first thing to notice

exactly where you stand on every control, in real-time.

[Show compliance score, control status, upcoming deadlines]
```

**Stop 2: Control Mapping (5 minutes)**
```
"Let's look at how we handle the actual compliance requirements. 
[Click into Controls]

You can see we've mapped every control across SOC 2, ISO 27001, 
and GDPR. But here's the key—you don't have to do all of them

Notice how when you select a framework, we automatically identify
```

**Stop 3: Evidence Automation (8 minutes)**

**Stop 4: Policy Management (5 minutes)**
```
"Now let's talk about policies. Most companies either buy generic

writing their own.

[Navigate to Policies]

We give you pre-built policies that are actually tailored to your
```

**Closing & Next Steps (3 minutes)**
```
"That's the core of CertFast. Before we wrap up, I want to check:

1. Did this address the [primary pain point] you mentioned?
2. What questions do you have?
3. Is there anything I showed that you want to see again?

[Answer questions]

Based on what you've seen, what would be the next step from your 
perspective?"

[Let them suggest, then align]

"That makes sense. Typically from here, companies either want to 
see [Option A: technical deep-dive with their team] or [Option B: 
proposal with implementation timeline]. Which sounds more useful?"

[Schedule next step]

"Great, I'll send you a calendar invite and [relevant resources]. 
Is there anyone else from your team who should join [next step]?"
```

### 4.3 Demo Best Practices

**Do:**
- Tailor the demo to their specific pain points
- Use their company name and context when possible
- Ask "Is this relevant to your situation?" regularly
- Show, don't tell—live demo beats slides
- Leave time for questions
- End with clear next steps

**Don't:**
- Demo features they didn't ask about
- Read from a script—be conversational
- Ignore technical stakeholders' questions
- Rush through to fit everything in
- Leave without scheduling a follow-up

## 5. Objection Handling Guide

### 5.1 Pricing Objections

**"Your price is too high."**
```
"I understand budget is always a consideration. Let me ask—when 
you say 'too high,' are you comparing us to a specific alternative, 
or is there a budget constraint you're working within?

[If comparing to DIY/consultants]
Most companies find that when they factor in the fully-loaded cost 
of doing this manually—engineering time, consultant fees, the delay 
in closing enterprise deals—we're actually significantly cheaper. 
One customer told us they calculated their DIY attempt was costing 
them €50K in engineering time alone.

[If budget constraint]
What budget are you working with? We have some flexibility in 
payment terms, and occasionally we can structure the deal to fit 
your fiscal year."
```

**"We can just do this ourselves/with a consultant."**
```
"You absolutely can—that's a valid option. Many companies start 
that way. Can I ask how long you've been considering that approach, 
and what's held you back so far?

[Listen]

What we typically see is that DIY or consultants get you the 
policies, but they don't solve the ongoing evidence collection 
problem. Compliance isn't a one-time project—it's continuous. 
That's where companies get stuck after month 3 or 4.

Would it be helpful if I connected you with a customer who tried 
the consultant route first and then switched to us? They could 
tell you what the difference was like."
```

### 5.2 Timing Objections

**"We're not ready for this yet."**
```
"Fair enough—timing matters. Can you help me understand what's 
driving that? Is it that compliance isn't a priority right now, 
or are there other things that need to happen first?

[If not a priority]
What would need to change for it to become a priority? We've 
found that most companies wait until they lose a deal or get 
pressure from a big prospect. Is that what you're waiting for?

[If dependencies]
That makes sense. What are the milestones you're waiting for? 
Sometimes we can help you get started in parallel so you're 
ready when those things happen."
```

**"We need to wait until after [event]."**
```
"I understand [event] is the focus right now. Can I ask—when 
do you expect that to wrap up? And what's the compliance timeline 
you're working toward after that?

[Calculate if timeline works]

Based on what you're saying, if you want to be compliant by 
[target date], you'd need to start [X weeks] before that. Does 
that fit with your plan?

What if we did a lightweight onboarding now—just getting your 
account set up and policies drafted—so you're ready to hit the 
ground running after [event]? No additional cost, just saves 
you time later."
```

### 5.3 Product/Competition Objections

**"We're already looking at [competitor]."**
```
"Great—[competitor] is a solid option. Can I ask what attracted 
you to them? Understanding that will help me give you a fair 
comparison.

[Listen, don't interrupt]

Those are valid points. Where we've seen companies choose us 
over [competitor] is typically [specific differentiator relevant 
to their stated priorities]. For example, [specific customer 
example].

Would it be helpful to see a side-by-side on the specific things 
that matter most to you?"
```

**"We need [feature] that you don't have."**
```
"Thanks for flagging that. Can you tell me more about how you'd 
use [feature] and what problem it solves for you?

[If we have it]
Actually, we do have that—let me show you. It might not have 
been obvious from my demo.

[If we don't have it]
That's not something we have today. Can I ask—is that a 
deal-breaker, or is it a nice-to-have? 

[If deal-breaker]
I'd rather be honest with you—if that's critical and we don't 
have it, we might not be the right fit right now. That said, 
it's on our roadmap for [timeline]. Would that work, or do you 
need it immediately?

[If nice-to-have]
Got it. We don't have that today, but we are planning it for 
[timeline]. In the meantime, most customers handle that by 
[workaround]. Would that work for your situation?"
```

**"We're worried about putting our compliance data in a third-party tool."**
```
"That's a completely valid concern—we hear it a lot, especially 
from security-conscious companies. In fact, it's exactly the 
same concern your customers have about trusting you.

Here's how we address it:
- We're SOC 2 Type II certified ourselves
- All data is encrypted at rest and in transit
- We host in [region] with [certifications]
- We can provide our security documentation and penetration test results
- We offer data processing agreements that meet GDPR requirements

Would it help if I connected you with our security team for a 
tech deep-dive? They can answer any specific questions about 
our infrastructure."
```

### 5.4 Process Objections

**"I need to check with my team/boss/board."**
```
"Of course—this is a decision that affects multiple people. Can 
you tell me who else needs to be involved and what they'll want 
to know?

[Map the buying committee]

Would it be helpful if I joined a call with [decision maker] 
to answer their questions directly? I can tailor the conversation 
to what they care about—whether that's technical implementation, 
ROI, timeline, or risk.

Also, I can send you a one-pager that summarizes the business 
case. Would that be useful for getting internal alignment?"
```

**"The implementation seems like a lot of work."**
```
"I get that concern—it can feel overwhelming. Let me break down 
what implementation actually looks like:

Week 1: Account setup, connect integrations (2-3 hours of your time)
Week 2-3: Policy customization (we do the heavy lifting, you review)
Week 4+: Evidence starts collecting automatically

The key is that we don't need you to stop everything else. Most 
customers spend 3-5 hours per week during the first month, then 
it drops to 30 minutes per week for ongoing maintenance.

And you're not doing this alone—we have customer success managers 
who guide you through each step. Would it help to talk to one of 
them about what the experience is actually like?"
```

## 7. Competitive Battle Cards

### 7.1 Drata

**The Competition**: US-based compliance automation platform, well-funded ($200M+ raised)

**Their Strengths**:
- Strong brand recognition in US market
- Large integration library
- Well-designed user interface

**Their Weaknesses**:
- Limited European presence (US-focused sales/support)
- Pricing often 2-3x higher for EU companies
- Less familiarity with European frameworks (GDPR nuances)
- No EU data residency options

**How We Win**:
- "Built for European companies from day one"
- GDPR-native architecture
- EU-based support team (timezone/language)
- 40% lower total cost of ownership
- Faster implementation for EU tech stacks

**Key Phrase**: *"Drata is a great product if you're a US company with US customers. If you're European, you need a partner who understands European compliance, European auditors, and European data requirements."*

### 7.2 Vanta

**The Competition**: Another well-funded US compliance platform (Unicorn status)

**Their Strengths**:
- Market leader in US SMB compliance
- Strong integrations with modern dev tools
- Good brand/marketing

**Their Weaknesses**:
- Expensive for what you get
- US-centric auditor network
- Limited flexibility in framework combinations
- Support primarily US timezone

**How We Win**:
- "European compliance expertise vs. US generic approach"
- Local auditor partnerships in major EU markets
- More flexible pricing for growing startups
- Better support for multi-framework (SOC 2 + ISO 27001 + GDPR together)

**Key Phrase**: *"Vanta optimized for the US startup journey. We optimized for the European journey—different auditors, different data requirements, different buyer expectations."*

### 7.3 Compliance Consultants (Big 4, Boutique)

**The Competition**: Traditional consulting firms (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG, or local boutiques)

**Their Strengths**:
- Brand credibility
- Deep audit experience
- Relationships with auditors

**Their Weaknesses**:
- Extremely expensive (€50K-€200K+ for initial certification)
- Slow (6-12 month engagements)
- Don't provide ongoing compliance
- Heavy dependency on their timeline
- Minimal technology/automation

**How We Win**:
- "Consultants charge €X and take Y months. We get you to the same 
  certification in Z weeks for €A—and then keep you compliant continuously."
- Technology-first vs. manual
- Ongoing value vs. one-time project

**Key Phrase**: *"Consultants will get you certified once. We get you certified and keep you compliant. Which do you think your auditors and customers prefer?"*

### 7.4 DIY / Spreadsheet Approach

**The Competition**: Doing it internally with spreadsheets, Google Docs, manual processes

**Their Strengths**:
- Lowest upfront cost
- Full control over process
- No vendor dependency

**Their Weaknesses**:
- Massive time sink (200-400 hours estimated)
- High failure rate (60%+ of DIY attempts fail first audit)
- No ongoing monitoring
- Hard to prove continuous compliance
- Difficult to scale across frameworks

**How We Win**:
- "What's your engineering team's time worth? Most companies underestimate 
  the time commitment by 3x."
- "DIY has a 40% first-time pass rate. Our customers have a 95% pass rate."
- Opportunity cost of delayed enterprise deals

**Key Phrase**: *"You can absolutely do this yourself—just like you could build your own CRM or email tool. The question is whether that's the best use of your team's time when you have product-market fit to scale."*

## 8. Closing Techniques

### 8.1 Trial Close Throughout

Don't wait until the end to gauge readiness. Use trial closes at each stage:

**Discovery:**
- "If we could solve [pain point], would that get you closer to your goal?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how important is solving this in the next 90 days?"

**Demo:**
- "Is this the kind of solution you had in mind?"
- "Can you see your team using this workflow?"

**Proposal:**
- "Does this investment fit within your budget expectations?"
- "If we can meet at [price], would you be ready to move forward?"

### 8.2 Closing Strategies

**The Assumptive Close**
```
"Based on everything we've discussed, it sounds like CertFast is 
the right solution for [Company]. Let's get you started with 
implementation on [date]. I'll send over the agreement now—
can you review it and get back to me by [day]?"
```

**The Alternative Close**
```
"Would you prefer to start with SOC 2 only, or do the bundled 
SOC 2 + ISO 27001 package? The bundle saves you 20% and most 
companies end up needing both anyway."
```

**The Urgency Close (use honestly)**
```
"You mentioned you have a big prospect waiting on your SOC 2. 
If we start implementation this week, you'll be audit-ready 
by [date]. If we wait until next month, that pushes to [later 
date] and you might miss that deal. Does starting this week 
make sense?"
```

**The Summary Close**
```
"Let me summarize what we're solving: [pain point 1], [pain 
point 2], and [pain point 3]. The investment is €[X] to get 
you compliant by [date]. Is there anything stopping us from 
moving forward today?"
```

### 8.3 Handling "I Need to Think About It"

```
"I completely understand—this is an important decision. Can I 
ask what specifically you need to think through? Is it the 
investment, the timing, or something about the solution itself?

[Address specific concern]

What would you need to see or know to feel confident moving 
forward? I want to make sure you have everything you need."
```

### 8.4 The Final Push

If the deal is stuck:

```
"I've enjoyed working with you on this, [Name]. I want to be 
respectful of your time and process. At the same time, I know 
you have [timeline pressure/pain point] and waiting has a cost.

Can I suggest a next step that works for both of us? [Specific 
action with deadline]. Does that feel fair?"
```
